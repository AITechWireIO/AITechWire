Scaramucci Defends Trump Amid Finance Scrutiny

Anthony Scaramucci pushes back on WSJ criticism of Trump family crypto dealings and questions dementia claims about a key figure. Fuels debate on political influence in markets.

AI TechWire · July 03, 2026 · 5 min read
Photo by rupixen / Unsplash

Washington's crypto entanglements don't disappear just because the market bounces. As Bitcoin clawed its way back to $61,600 this week — up 6.5% from Tuesday's low of $57,750 — the political noise surrounding Trump-linked digital assets refuses to quiet down. Anthony Scaramucci is back at the microphone, and the crypto market is listening, even if it isn't fully convinced.

Scaramucci Steps Into the Fray

Former White House Communications Director Anthony Scaramucci has been making the rounds pushing back hard on Wall Street Journal criticism targeting Trump family crypto dealings, while simultaneously casting doubt on dementia claims circulating about a key political figure. The intervention is quintessential Scaramucci: loud, combative, and deliberately calculated to shift the narrative.

For crypto markets, the timing matters enormously. Trump-linked digital asset ventures have become a barometer for policy expectations — a real-time sentiment gauge on whether the administration will tilt regulatory frameworks in favor of crypto or quietly let enforcement tighten. When a figure as conspicuous as Scaramucci wades into a media firefight defending those interests, traders pay attention.

But attention isn't the same as conviction. And right now, conviction is precisely what's missing from this market.

"Put options, derivative contracts offering protection against price slides, continue to trade at a premium to calls, or bullish contracts."

- CoinDesk, July 3, 2026

A Bounce Without Believers

The headline numbers look encouraging. Bitcoin recovered to $61,928 on July 3, and the broader crypto market is showing signs of life. Weak U.S. jobs data has lowered expectations for a Federal Reserve rate hike, lifting Nasdaq 100 futures by 1.9% and giving crypto bulls a macro tailwind they've been desperate for. Uniswap surged 11% on doubled trading volume after securing a link-up with Robinhood, giving altcoin traders at least one clean momentum story to chase.

Ether dominated the derivatives picture, accounting for $160 million of the $417 million in 24-hour liquidations as heavily bearish positioning was squeezed out. ETH open interest climbed to its highest since June 10, alongside bullish funding rates and the strongest cumulative volume delta among major assets.

And yet, the options market is telling a different story entirely. On Deribit, Bitcoin's one-week, 25-delta put-call skew sits at around 16% — meaning traders are still paying a significant premium to protect against downside rather than bet on upside. Bears still hold the structural advantage, and no amount of political theater from Scaramucci changes that technical reality.

Political Influence and Market Sentiment: An Uncomfortable Marriage

Here's the uncomfortable truth that the Scaramucci intervention forces into the open: Trump-linked crypto has become inextricably politicized, and that politicization carries a risk premium that no bullish macro data point can fully neutralize.

When scrutiny from outlets like the Wall Street Journal targets the financial dealings of politically connected crypto ventures, it creates a specific kind of market anxiety. It's not just reputational. It's regulatory. It signals that these assets could become flash points for enforcement action, legislative scrutiny, or simply the kind of sustained negative press coverage that erodes retail confidence over time.

Scaramucci's pushback, however aggressive, doesn't make that scrutiny disappear. If anything, it amplifies the story — drawing more eyeballs to the underlying questions about political influence in crypto markets, not fewer.

The debate he's fueling is legitimate and consequential: To what degree are Trump-linked crypto assets functioning as policy proxies? And if administration positions shift on crypto regulation, do holders of those assets benefit in ways that blur the line between investment and political access? These are questions that the WSJ is asking, that Scaramucci is swatting away, and that the market is quietly pricing into its put-heavy options positioning.

What Traders Should Watch

The macro setup is genuinely improving. Receding Fed rate-hike risk is real, and the jobs data that drove this week's recovery reflects a softening economy that gives the central bank cover to stay put. That's a constructive backdrop for risk assets broadly, crypto included.

But the political overhang on Trump-linked crypto specifically — and on broader policy expectations for the sector — remains unresolved. Scaramucci's media offensive may generate short-term noise and even a sentiment bump among retail participants who read his defense as a signal of inside confidence. Sophisticated options traders, however, are not buying it. The put premium tells you everything: the market respects the bounce, but it hasn't abandoned its hedges.

With Bitcoin hovering near $61,928 and ETH open interest at its highest since June 10, the bulls have a technical window. Whether they can push through it will depend less on what Scaramucci says next and more on whether Washington's crypto entanglements remain a sideshow — or escalate into the main event.

The smart money is hedged. The political noise is loud. Those two facts are not unrelated.
